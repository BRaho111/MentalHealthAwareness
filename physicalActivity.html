Boost Your Mood with Exercise

Research from Mayo Clinic has proven that teens who perform regular physical activity tend to have improved mental health. Exercise stimulates the production of endorphins. Endorphins are a natural mood lifter in our brains. They reduce feelings of stress and anxiety. Moderate activities include walking, jogging, or playing a sport at a moderate pace. These can improve your mood, boost your self-confidence, and promote better sleep. Mayo Clinic focuses on consistent physical activity as a vital and effective way to manage stress and relieve symptoms of depression and anxiety in teenagers, supporting a life-long of improved mental well-being.
